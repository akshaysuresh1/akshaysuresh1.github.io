Searches for Periodic Extraterrestrial Technosignatures

The search for extraterrestrial intelligence (SETI) is an active quest to find evidence of advanced alien life through signatures of their technologies. Surveys for such “technosignatures” enable astronomers to probe the fundamental question of existence of life out to vast interstellar distances, thereby complementing solar system exploration missions and searches for molecules of life in exoplanet atmospheres.

Radio SETI campaigns have mainly targeted the discovery of narrowband continuous wave transmissions and one-off unexplained events from alien worlds. The former signal category with all power concentrated into a few hertz is a characteristic transmission from human technologies unseen in the natural world.

In contrast to the above signal classes, regularly repeating wideband signals offer an energetically efficient means of signal transmission. Here, we developed blipss, an open-source CPU-based package to enable searches for repetitive artificial patterns of extraterrestrial origin. Using blipss, we monitored the central 6’ of our galaxy for series of pulses repeating every 11 to 100 seconds and spread across a few kilohertz in bandwidth, analogous to radar pulses. Our searches thus expand the scope of radio SETI to include periodic pulsating signals, a historically underexplored form of potential extraterrestrial signal transmission.
